## Design Context

### Users
System administrators and developers who need to diagnose disk space issues quickly, often on remote servers via SSH. They value speed, accuracy, and clear information density.

### Brand Personality
Industrial, Utilitarian, Efficient. The interface should feel like a reliable piece of machinery: precise, robust, and no-nonsense.

### Aesthetic Direction
Industrial/Utilitarian with a dark theme. High contrast for readability, using standard terminal colors (ANSI) for maximum compatibility. Avoids unnecessary decoration, focusing on data visualization (proportional bars) and clear status indicators.

### Design Principles
1. **Information Density**: Maximize useful data without cluttering the screen.
2. **Visual Hierarchy**: Use brightness and color (sparingly) to guide the eye to the largest offenders.
3. **Robustness**: Ensure the TUI remains usable even on small terminals or high-latency connections.
4. **Predictability**: Consistent key bindings and layout that follow standard shell utility patterns (like `ncdu` or `htop`).
